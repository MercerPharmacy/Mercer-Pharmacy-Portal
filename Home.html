FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

For a comprehensive review of financial aid, please visit the Office of Student Financial Planning website.

If you are starting before August 2026, you will need to complete the 2025-2026 FAFSA using your 2021 tax information. If you are starting in or after August 2026, you will need to complete the 2026-2027 FAFSA using your 2024 tax information.

It takes 2-5 business days for Mercer University to receive your FAFSA after you complete it. After you submit a FAFSA, Reach out to your academic advisor to discuss what courses you will need to take, and ensure that you will be at least half time in order to receive federal student aid. You will receive an email from us once we receive your FAFSA. At that point, we will then be able to work on your financial aid package.

As a graduate student, the only source of federal student aid through the FAFSA is in the form of loans. Grants and scholarships are sometimes offered through your program’s departments and we encourage inquiring within. Financial Planning also has a database of outside scholarships students can apply for, on our webpage.

Aid offerings must be accepted or declined on the MyMercer portal to show on your billing statement.

Instructional guides are available to show you how: https://financialaid.mercer.edu/how-to-guides/how-to-accept-your-financial-aid-package.cfm.

Students are offered the maximum amount they are eligible to receive in a federal unsubsidized loan for the year. If there is room left in a student’s Cost of Attendance, you may apply for a Federal Direct Graduate Plus Loan to bridge the gap, not to exceed the annual Cost of Attendance.
